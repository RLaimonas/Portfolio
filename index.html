Welcome to My Portfolio

   This is the beta version of my portfolio. Initially, I created it as a personal repository to collect information from my various projects that is otherwise scattered. It's a tool for me to quickly recall and refer to my past works. However, as the development progressed, I realized its potential as a platform to publicly showcase my skills and knowledge. Hence, I decided to make it accessible to all.
   Here, you’ll find a collection of various projects I’ve been involved in. The portfolio includes a mix of both major and minor projects. Some of the detailed and actual projects are excluded because they were developed for commercial purposes or are under confidentiality agreements
    This portfolio is a work in progress. I am in the process of retrieving archives of my older projects to include here. Moving forward, my intention is to update this page in real-time as projects progress, rather than after their completion – though the feasibility of this approach remains to be tested:)

Escape Room Electric Boxes

Start Date: Apr 2023 | Duration: 2 weeks | Status: Completed

Skills: Python Scripting, Design, Restoration

I received a request to create three boxes styled to look old, where players enter a combination. When the right combination is entered, the Raspberry Pi Pico controller sends a command to the game master's PC via USB. I also modified an old phone to let the game master communicate with players through a PC, using a 3.5mm aux connection. Lithophane Image

Start Date: Mar 2020 | Duration: 1 Month | Status: Completed

Skills: 3D Printing, Design, Woodworking

I crafted this "3D" image as a special gift. The image was converted to G-code using online tools and printed with PLA plastic. To ensure an even light source I used a tablet screen backlight. The image appears when light shines through the plastic; variations in thickness create different light intensities, revealing the image. It's powered by a 5Ah Li-po battery with a magnetic charge connection, which can last up to 56 hours. Read More...

Start Date: Feb 2019 | Duration: 5 Months | Status: Prototype Completed

Skills: Battery management, Electronics, Circuit Design, C++ Programming

I needed a device for testing refurbished 18650 Li-ion batteries and modified Brett Watt's VortexIT project to be a standalone device, adding features like multiple charge/discharge cycles for accurate capacity testing and a cell revive mode for safely recovering deep discharged cells. 90's Boombox Revival

Start Date: Jan 2018 | Duration: 2 Months | Status: Completed

Skills: Restoration, Electronics, Audio Engineering

Many of us have had the desire to blast music at full volume, but I aspired to do so with a touch of elegance. I purchased a broken Loewe 8694 radio, aiming to restore its classic charm. While its exterior remained untouched, the interior underwent a complete transformation. I installed laser-cut fittings for bigger speakers, added D-class amplifiers, a Bluetooth receiver, and a battery. Even old stereo level indications came back to live. Read More...
